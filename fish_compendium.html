📏 Size6–15 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 6 lbs

📍 HabitatLakes, rivers, ponds
📏 Size12–30 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 80 lbs

📏 Size12–24 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 50 lbs
⭐ Points+15
🎣 Fun Fact: Named for their whisker-like barbels. They use taste buds covering their entire body to find food in murky water.

Channel Catfish

📍 HabitatRivers & reservoirs
📏 Size12–24 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 58 lbs
⭐ Points+25
🎣 Fun Fact: Michigan's most common catfish species. They are nocturnal hunters with over 27,000 taste buds on their body.

⚖️ WeightUp to 5 lbs

🎣 Fun Fact: Travel in large schools and are most active at dawn and dusk. One of the best-tasting freshwater fish in Michigan.

⚖️ WeightUp to 22 lbs

📏 Size18–45 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 46 lbs

🎣 Fun Fact: An apex predator with a mouth full of razor-sharp teeth. Known to ambush prey from hiding in aquatic vegetation.

📏 Size5–12 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 3 lbs
⭐ Points+10
🎣 Fun Fact: A Great Lakes staple. Travels in large schools and is one of Michigan's most commercially important fish species.
Rare

Rainbow Trout

📍 HabitatCold rivers & Lake Michigan
📏 Size12–30 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 30 lbs
⭐ Points+60
🎣 Fun Fact: When they migrate from the Great Lakes into rivers to spawn, they're called Steelhead — one of Michigan's most sought-after game fish.

Rock Bass

📍 HabitatRocky lake shores
📏 Size6–10 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 3 lbs
⭐ Points+10
🎣 Fun Fact: Known as the 'redeye' for their distinctive red eyes. They can change color slightly to blend with their rocky surroundings.
Rare

Chinook Salmon

📍 HabitatLake Michigan tributaries
📏 Size24–40 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 100 lbs
⭐ Points+75
🎣 Fun Fact: The largest Pacific salmon species. Stocked in Lake Michigan since 1967 to control alewife populations — now a legendary sport fish.
Uncommon

Freshwater Drum (Sheephead)

📍 HabitatSandy & gravelly bottoms
📏 Size10–20 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 54 lbs
⭐ Points+20
🎣 Fun Fact: The only freshwater drum in North America. Makes a deep rumbling sound using muscles that vibrate against its swim bladder.
Uncommon

Smallmouth Bass

📍 HabitatClear, rocky streams & lakes
📏 Size10–20 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 12 lbs
⭐ Points+35
🎣 Fun Fact: Pound for pound considered one of the hardest-fighting freshwater fish. Prefers cold, clear water with rocky or gravel bottoms.
Rare

Walleye

📍 HabitatDeep lakes & rivers
📏 Size12–30 in
⚖️ WeightUp to 25 lbs
⭐ Points+70
🎣 Fun Fact: Michigan's most popular eating fish. Their large, glassy eyes give them excellent night vision — they're most active at dusk and dawn.
